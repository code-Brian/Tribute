Marie Curie

A Brief History

1867

Marie was born on November 7th in Warsaw.

1891

Marie leaves Poland to study in Paris at the Sorbonne where she obtained Licenciateships in Physics and the Mathematical Sciences.

1894

She meets Pierre Curie, Professor in the School of Physics

1895

Marie and Pierre are married.

1903

Marie gains her Doctor of Science degree as Head of the Physics Labaroatory at the Sorbonne.

1906

Following Pierre's tragic death, Marie takes his place as Professor of General Physics in the Faculty of Sciences, the first time a woman had held this position.

1914

Marie is appointed Director of the Curie Laboratory in the Radium Institute of the University of Paris.

1921

President Harding of the United States, on behalf of the women of America, presented her with one gram of radium in recognition of her service to science.

1929

President Hoover of the United States presents her a gift of $50,000, donated by American friends of sceince, to purchase radium for use in the laboratory in Warsaw.

1934

After a brief illness, Marie dies in Savoy, France on July 4th.
